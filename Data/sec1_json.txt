The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 reveals a period of notable economic growth and resilience. In 2019, the GRP stood at approximately $244.28 billion, and by 2023, it had risen to about $308.71 billion, marking an impressive increase of $64.43 billion over five years. This growth trajectory underscores the region's economic vitality, even amidst the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.

The annual GRP growth paints a picture of recovery and expansion. From 2019 to 2020, the GRP experienced a modest rise to $244.82 billion, reflecting the initial impact of the pandemic. However, the subsequent year saw a significant rebound, with the GRP climbing to $268.87 billion in 2021. This upward trend continued, reaching $296.68 billion in 2022 and culminating at $308.71 billion in 2023. Such figures highlight the region's ability to bounce back and sustain growth.

Per capita GRP also saw a substantial increase, rising from approximately $73,347 in 2019 to $94,916 in 2023. This growth indicates enhanced economic productivity per individual, suggesting improvements in the overall economic health of the region. Notably, San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 surpassed both the California state average of $93,800 and the national average of $77,366, underscoring the county's strong economic performance relative to broader benchmarks.

Key sectors driving this economic expansion include Government, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Manufacturing. The Government sector emerged as the largest contributor, adding $52.92 billion to the GRP in 2023. Meanwhile, the Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector contributed $37.04 billion, and Manufacturing accounted for $31.67 billion, highlighting the diverse economic base supporting the region's growth.

The data reflects San Diego County's economic resilience and adaptability, particularly in the face of the pandemic. The steady increase in both GRP and per capita GRP suggests a robust recovery and a positive economic outlook. Overall, the period from 2019 to 2023 was marked by significant economic achievements, positioning San Diego County as a strong performer on both state and national levels.
