The Gross Regional Product (GRP) is a key economic indicator that measures the economic output of a region. For San Diego County, we will analyze the GRP data from 2019 to 2023, focusing on the total GRP, industry contributions, and per capita GRP.

### Total GRP Analysis (2019-2023)

1. **2019**: The total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
2. **2020**: The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion, despite the economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
3. **2021**: A significant increase in GRP was observed, reaching approximately $268.87 billion, indicating economic recovery and growth.
4. **2022**: The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
5. **2023**: The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion, reflecting sustained economic growth.

### Industry Contributions to GRP

- **Government**: Consistently the largest contributor to the GRP, with contributions increasing from approximately $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
- **Manufacturing**: Another major contributor, with its GRP increasing from approximately $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023.
- **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services**: This sector showed significant growth, with its GRP rising from approximately $28.13 billion in 2019 to $37.04 billion in 2023.
- **Health Care and Social Assistance**: This sector also saw growth, with its GRP increasing from approximately $15.38 billion in 2019 to $20.21 billion in 2023.
- **Finance and Insurance**: This sector's GRP increased from approximately $14.67 billion in 2019 to $19.50 billion in 2023.

### Per Capita GRP Analysis

- **2019**: The per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
- **2020**: It increased slightly to approximately $74,278.
- **2021**: A notable increase to approximately $82,100.
- **2022**: The per capita GRP rose to approximately $90,557.
- **2023**: It further increased to approximately $94,916.

### Conclusion

San Diego County's economy has shown resilience and growth over the past five years, with a steady increase in both total and per capita GRP. The government, manufacturing, and professional services sectors have been key drivers of this growth. The increase in per capita GRP indicates that the economic benefits are being distributed among the population, contributing to the overall economic well-being of the region.
