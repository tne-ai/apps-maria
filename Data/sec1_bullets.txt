San Diego County's Gross Regional Product (GRP) provides a comprehensive view of the economic output of the region. Here's a breakdown of the GRP for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023, based on the provided data:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a growth of approximately 26.4% over the five-year period.

2. **Yearly GRP Figures**:
   - **2019**: $244.28 billion
   - **2020**: $244.82 billion
   - **2021**: $268.87 billion
   - **2022**: $296.68 billion
   - **2023**: $308.71 billion

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP in 2019 was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates a per capita growth of approximately 29.4% over the five-year period.

4. **Industry Contributions**:
   - The largest contributors to the GRP in 2023 were:
     - Government: $52.92 billion
     - Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services: $37.04 billion
     - Manufacturing: $31.67 billion
     - Health Care and Social Assistance: $20.21 billion
     - Finance and Insurance: $19.50 billion

5. **Trends and Observations**:
   - The GRP saw a slight dip in 2020, likely due to the economic impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic, but rebounded strongly in the subsequent years.
   - The Government sector consistently remained the largest contributor to the GRP throughout the years.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector showed significant growth, reflecting the region's strong emphasis on innovation and technology.

Overall, San Diego County's economy has demonstrated resilience and growth, with significant contributions from key sectors such as government, professional services, and manufacturing. The increase in per capita GRP also suggests an improvement in economic productivity and living standards in the region.
