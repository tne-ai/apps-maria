### Key Observations

1. **Growth in Total GRP**: 
   - From 2019 to 2023, San Diego County's total GRP increased from approximately $244.28 billion to $308.71 billion. This represents a growth of about 26.4% over the five-year period.

2. **Growth in GRP Per Capita**:
   - The GRP per capita also showed a significant increase from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023, marking a growth of approximately 29.4%.

3. **Yearly Trends**:
   - The GRP saw a steady increase each year, with the most significant jump occurring between 2021 and 2022, where the total GRP increased by approximately $27.81 billion, and the per capita GRP increased by $8,457.15.

4. **Economic Resilience**:
   - Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, San Diego County's GRP managed to grow slightly from 2019 to 2020, indicating economic resilience.

5. **Population Impact**:
   - The population of San Diego County slightly decreased from 3,330,458 in 2019 to 3,252,468 in 2023, which may have contributed to the increase in GRP per capita as the total GRP continued to rise.

### Conclusion

San Diego County has demonstrated robust economic growth over the past five years, with both total GRP and GRP per capita showing significant increases. This growth reflects the county's ability to adapt and thrive even in challenging economic conditions.
