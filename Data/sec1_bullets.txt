### Analysis of San Diego County's GRP

#### Total GRP (in dollars)
- **2019**: $244,278,846,457.50
- **2020**: $244,822,303,264.04
- **2021**: $268,873,550,391.04
- **2022**: $296,683,894,891.99
- **2023**: $308,710,843,090.86

#### GRP Per Capita
- **2019**: $73,346.92
- **2020**: $74,277.60
- **2021**: $82,099.95
- **2022**: $90,557.10
- **2023**: $94,915.87

### Key Observations

1. **Growth in Total GRP**: 
   - From 2019 to 2023, San Diego County's total GRP increased from approximately $244.28 billion to $308.71 billion. This represents a growth of about 26.4% over the five-year period.
   - The most significant year-over-year increase occurred between 2021 and 2022, with an increase of approximately $27.81 billion.

2. **Growth in GRP Per Capita**:
   - The GRP per capita also showed a consistent upward trend, increasing from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023.
   - This represents a growth of approximately 29.4% over the five-year period.
   - The largest increase in GRP per capita occurred between 2021 and 2022, with an increase of $8,457.15.

3. **Impact of Population Changes**:
   - The population of San Diego County slightly decreased from 3,330,458 in 2019 to 3,252,468 in 2023. This decrease in population, coupled with the increase in total GRP, contributed to the rise in GRP per capita.

### Conclusion

San Diego County's economy has shown robust growth over the past five years, both in terms of total GRP and GRP per capita. The consistent increase in GRP per capita suggests that the economic output per resident has improved, indicating a strengthening economy. Despite a slight decrease in population, the county's economic performance has been strong, with significant contributions from various industries driving this growth.
