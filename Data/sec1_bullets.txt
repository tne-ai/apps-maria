The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $73,346.92
   - Key Industries: Government ($45.19 billion), Manufacturing ($28.26 billion), and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services ($28.13 billion) were the top contributors.

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $74,277.60
   - Impact of COVID-19: The Accommodation and Food Services industry saw a significant decline from $9.02 billion in 2019 to $6.53 billion in 2020, reflecting the pandemic's impact.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $82,099.95
   - Recovery Phase: Industries like Accommodation and Food Services rebounded to $8.80 billion, and Manufacturing increased to $31.28 billion, indicating recovery from the pandemic.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $90,557.10
   - Continued Growth: The Government sector remained the largest contributor at $50.56 billion, while Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services grew to $35.74 billion.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $94,915.87
   - Sustained Growth: The Information sector saw significant growth, reaching $14.90 billion, and the Government sector continued to lead at $52.92 billion.

**Overall Trends**:
- The GRP of San Diego County has shown a steady increase from 2019 to 2023, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 6.0%.
- The per capita GRP also increased, reflecting both population stability and economic growth.
- Key sectors such as Government, Manufacturing, and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services have consistently been major contributors to the GRP.
- The impact of the COVID-19 pandemic was evident in 2020, but the economy showed resilience and recovery in subsequent years.

This analysis highlights the economic resilience and growth of San Diego County over the past five years, driven by diverse and robust industry sectors.
