The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County provides a comprehensive overview of the economic output of the region. The data spans from 2019 to 2023, offering insights into the economic trends and sectoral contributions over these years.

### Overview of San Diego County's GRP (2019-2023)

1. **Total GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a growth of approximately 26.4% over the five-year period.

2. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP in 2019 was $73,346.92.
   - By 2023, it increased to $94,915.87.
   - This indicates a growth of approximately 29.4% in per capita GRP over the period.

3. **Sectoral Contributions**:
   - **Government**: Consistently the largest contributor, with $45.19 billion in 2019, growing to $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - **Manufacturing**: Another significant sector, contributing $28.26 billion in 2019 and $31.67 billion in 2023.
   - **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services**: Grew from $28.13 billion in 2019 to $37.04 billion in 2023.
   - **Health Care and Social Assistance**: Increased from $15.38 billion in 2019 to $20.21 billion in 2023.
   - **Finance and Insurance**: Saw growth from $14.67 billion in 2019 to $19.50 billion in 2023.

4. **Impact of COVID-19 (2020)**:
   - The GRP in 2020 was $244.82 billion, showing a slight increase from 2019 despite the pandemic.
   - Sectors like Accommodation and Food Services and Arts, Entertainment, and Recreation experienced declines in 2020 but rebounded in subsequent years.

5. **Comparative Analysis**:
   - San Diego County's GRP per capita in 2023 ($94,915.87) is higher than the state average of California ($93,799.67) and significantly higher than the national average ($77,366.43).

### Conclusion

San Diego County's economy has shown resilience and growth over the past five years, with significant contributions from government, manufacturing, and professional services sectors. The increase in per capita GRP indicates improved economic productivity and prosperity for the region's residents. Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, the county's economy has rebounded strongly, with key sectors driving growth.
