The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the years. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $73,346.92
   - Key Industries: Government ($45.19 billion), Manufacturing ($28.26 billion), and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services ($28.13 billion).

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion (a slight increase from 2019)
   - Per Capita GRP: $74,277.60
   - Key Industries: Government ($46.58 billion), Manufacturing ($28.83 billion), and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services ($29.10 billion).
   - Notable Impact: The Accommodation and Food Services industry saw a significant decline from $9.02 billion in 2019 to $6.53 billion in 2020, likely due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion (an increase of approximately 9.8% from 2020)
   - Per Capita GRP: $82,099.95
   - Key Industries: Government ($49.23 billion), Manufacturing ($31.28 billion), and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services ($32.51 billion).
   - Recovery: The Accommodation and Food Services industry rebounded to $8.80 billion.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion (an increase of approximately 10.3% from 2021)
   - Per Capita GRP: $90,557.10
   - Key Industries: Government ($50.56 billion), Manufacturing ($33.50 billion), and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services ($35.74 billion).
   - Growth: The Accommodation and Food Services industry continued to grow, reaching $11.77 billion.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion (an increase of approximately 4.1% from 2022)
   - Per Capita GRP: $94,915.87
   - Key Industries: Government ($52.92 billion), Manufacturing ($31.67 billion), and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services ($37.04 billion).
   - Continued Growth: The Accommodation and Food Services industry further increased to $11.83 billion.

**Overall Trends**:
- The GRP of San Diego County has shown a steady increase from 2019 to 2023, with a notable recovery and growth post-2020.
- The Government sector consistently remains the largest contributor to the GRP, followed by Manufacturing and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services.
- The Accommodation and Food Services industry experienced a significant decline in 2020 due to the pandemic but has shown a strong recovery in subsequent years.

This analysis highlights the resilience and growth of San Diego County's economy over the past five years, with a strong recovery from the pandemic's impact.
