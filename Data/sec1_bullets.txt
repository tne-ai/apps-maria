**San Diego County's Gross Regional Product (GRP) Report**

**Overall Trends**

San Diego County's GRP has shown steady growth over the years, with some fluctuations. The total GRP has increased from approximately $298.9 billion in 2019 to $365.0 billion in 2023, representing a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.3%.

**Per Capita Analysis**

The per capita GRP in San Diego County has also demonstrated growth, rising from $75,788 in 2019 to $93,799 in 2023. This represents a CAGR of 4.5%. The per capita GRP is a useful metric for understanding the standard of living within the county.

**Sector Contributions**

The sector contributions to San Diego County's GRP are diverse, with the following sectors being the largest contributors:

* Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services: 14.1% of total GRP in 2023
* Manufacturing: 12.4% of total GRP in 2023
* Finance and Insurance: 11.4% of total GRP in 2023
* Health Care and Social Assistance: 10.3% of total GRP in 2023
* Retail Trade: 8.5% of total GRP in 2023

**Year-over-Year Changes**

The year-over-year changes in San Diego County's GRP are as follows:

* 2020: 0.9% decrease compared to 2019
* 2021: 11.8% increase compared to 2020
* 2022: 6.8% increase compared to 2021
* 2023: 3.2% increase compared to 2022

**Observations**

San Diego County's GRP has demonstrated resilience in the face of economic challenges, with a strong and diverse economy. The growth in the Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector is particularly notable, highlighting the county's strength in innovation and technology.

**Conclusion**

In conclusion, San Diego County's GRP has shown steady growth over the years, driven by a diverse range of sectors. The per capita GRP has also increased, indicating a rising standard of living within the county. The county's strong economy and innovative sectors position it for continued growth and success in the future.

**Key Takeaways**

* San Diego County's GRP has grown from $298.9 billion in 2019 to $365.0 billion in 2023, representing a CAGR of 4.3%.
* The per capita GRP has increased from $75,788 in 2019 to $93,799 in 2023, representing a CAGR of 4.5%.
* The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector is the largest contributor to the county's GRP, accounting for 14.1% of the total in 2023.
* The county's diverse economy and innovative sectors position it for continued growth and success in the future.
