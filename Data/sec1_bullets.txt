### San Diego County GRP Analysis (2019-2023)

#### Overview
The Gross Regional Product (GRP) is a measure of the economic output of a region. It is similar to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) but at a regional level. The GRP per capita is an important indicator as it reflects the average economic output per person in the region.

#### GRP Trends
1. **Total GRP (in dollars)**
   - 2019: $244.28 billion
   - 2020: $244.82 billion
   - 2021: $268.87 billion
   - 2022: $296.68 billion
   - 2023: $308.71 billion

2. **Population**
   - 2019: 3,330,458
   - 2020: 3,296,045
   - 2021: 3,274,954
   - 2022: 3,276,208
   - 2023: 3,252,468

3. **GRP Per Capita**
   - 2019: $73,346.92
   - 2020: $74,277.60
   - 2021: $82,099.95
   - 2022: $90,557.10
   - 2023: $94,915.87

#### Analysis
- **Growth in GRP**: San Diego County's GRP has shown a consistent increase from 2019 to 2023, with a notable jump between 2020 and 2021, and continued growth through 2023. This indicates a strong economic recovery and growth post-2020.
  
- **Population Trends**: The population of San Diego County has slightly decreased from 2019 to 2023, which may have contributed to the increase in GRP per capita as the economic output grew while the population slightly declined.

- **GRP Per Capita Growth**: The GRP per capita has increased significantly over the years, from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023. This suggests that the economic productivity per person in the county has improved, reflecting a robust economic environment.

#### Comparison with State and National Levels
- **California (State Level) GRP Per Capita**:
  - 2019: $75,788.64
  - 2023: $93,799.67

- **National (U.S.) GRP Per Capita**:
  - 2019: $63,753.57
  - 2023: $77,366.43

San Diego County's GRP per capita has consistently been below the state average but above the national average. This indicates that while San Diego County is performing well compared to the national level, there is still room for growth to match the state average.

### Conclusion
San Diego County has demonstrated strong economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant increases in both total GRP and GRP per capita. The county's economic performance is robust, with GRP per capita surpassing the national average, although it remains slightly below the state average. This growth reflects a positive economic trajectory for the region, suggesting a resilient and expanding economy.
