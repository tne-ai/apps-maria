The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the years. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Population: 3,330,458
   - GRP per Capita: $73,346.92

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Population: 3,296,045
   - GRP per Capita: $74,277.60
   - Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County's GRP saw a slight increase, reflecting resilience in certain sectors.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Population: 3,274,954
   - GRP per Capita: $82,099.95
   - The significant increase in GRP and GRP per capita suggests a strong economic recovery post-pandemic, with growth in industries such as manufacturing and professional services.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Population: 3,276,208
   - GRP per Capita: $90,557.10
   - Continued growth in GRP indicates robust economic performance, with contributions from sectors like healthcare, finance, and insurance.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Population: 3,252,468
   - GRP per Capita: $94,915.87
   - The GRP per capita reached its highest point in this period, reflecting increased productivity and economic output per resident.

**Key Observations**:
- The GRP of San Diego County increased by approximately 26.4% from 2019 to 2023.
- The GRP per capita rose by about 29.4% over the same period, indicating that the economic growth outpaced population growth.
- The data suggests that San Diego County's economy is diverse and resilient, with significant contributions from various sectors, including government, healthcare, and professional services.

Overall, San Diego County's economic performance from 2019 to 2023 demonstrates a strong and resilient economy, capable of recovering from external shocks and continuing to grow.
