The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County provides a comprehensive overview of the economic performance of the region. The GRP is a measure of the total economic output of the county, reflecting the value of all goods and services produced within the region. Here is a detailed analysis based on the provided data:

### Overview of San Diego County's GRP (2019-2023)

1. **Total GRP Growth:**
   - In 2019, the GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a growth of approximately 26.4% over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Figures:**
   - 2019: $244.28 billion
   - 2020: $244.82 billion
   - 2021: $268.87 billion
   - 2022: $296.68 billion
   - 2023: $308.71 billion

3. **Per Capita GRP:**
   - The per capita GRP in 2019 was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates a significant increase in economic productivity per person over the period.

4. **Industry Contributions:**
   - The largest contributors to the GRP in 2023 were:
     - Government: $52.92 billion
     - Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services: $37.04 billion
     - Manufacturing: $31.67 billion
     - Health Care and Social Assistance: $20.21 billion
     - Finance and Insurance: $19.50 billion

5. **Trends and Observations:**
   - The Government sector consistently remained the largest contributor to the GRP throughout the period.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector showed robust growth, reflecting the region's strong emphasis on innovation and technology.
   - The Manufacturing sector also demonstrated significant growth, indicating a strong industrial base in the county.
   - The Health Care and Social Assistance sector's growth highlights the increasing demand for health services in the region.

6. **Comparison with State and National Averages:**
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) was higher than the state average of California ($93,800) and significantly higher than the national average ($77,366).
   - This suggests that San Diego County is economically more productive on a per-person basis compared to both the state and national levels.

### Conclusion

San Diego County has demonstrated strong economic growth over the past five years, with significant contributions from key sectors such as government, professional services, and manufacturing. The increase in per capita GRP indicates enhanced productivity and economic prosperity for the region's residents. The county's economic performance surpasses both state and national averages, highlighting its role as a vital economic hub in California.
