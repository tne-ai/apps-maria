The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 provides insights into the economic performance of the region over this period. Here's a detailed breakdown:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total growth of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP saw a slight increase from $244.28 billion to $244.82 billion, indicating resilience despite potential economic challenges.
   - The GRP grew significantly from 2020 to 2021, reaching $268.87 billion, marking a recovery phase.
   - Continued growth was observed in 2022, with the GRP reaching $296.68 billion.
   - The upward trend persisted into 2023, with the GRP reaching $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates an increase in economic productivity per person over the period.

4. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP consistently outperformed the national average throughout the period.
   - In 2023, San Diego County's per capita GRP was $94,916, compared to California's $93,800 and the national average of $77,366.

5. **Sectoral Contributions**:
   - Key sectors contributing to the GRP included Government, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Manufacturing.
   - The Government sector consistently had the highest contribution, with $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector also showed strong performance, contributing $37.04 billion in 2023.
   - Manufacturing remained a significant contributor, with $31.67 billion in 2023.

6. **Economic Resilience and Recovery**:
   - The data indicates that San Diego County demonstrated economic resilience, particularly during the pandemic period (2020-2021), with a strong recovery in subsequent years.
   - The consistent growth in GRP and per capita GRP suggests a robust economic environment with increasing productivity and output.

In summary, San Diego County experienced substantial economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant contributions from key sectors and a strong per capita GRP performance relative to state and national levels. The region's economy showed resilience and recovery, particularly in the face of global economic challenges.
