The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the years. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Population: 3,330,458
   - GRP per Capita: $73,346.92

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Population: 3,296,045
   - GRP per Capita: $74,277.60
   - Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County's GRP saw a slight increase. The GRP per capita also increased, indicating that the economic output per person improved.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Population: 3,274,954
   - GRP per Capita: $82,099.95
   - The GRP saw a significant increase, reflecting a strong economic recovery post-pandemic. The GRP per capita also rose substantially, suggesting enhanced productivity and economic output.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Population: 3,276,208
   - GRP per Capita: $90,557.10
   - The growth trend continued with a notable increase in both total GRP and GRP per capita, indicating robust economic performance and increased economic activity in the region.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Population: 3,252,468
   - GRP per Capita: $94,915.87
   - The GRP reached its highest point in this period, with a continued increase in GRP per capita, suggesting sustained economic growth and development in San Diego County.

**Key Observations**:
- Over the five-year period, San Diego County's GRP increased by approximately 26.4%, from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023.
- The GRP per capita also saw a significant rise of about 29.4%, from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023.
- The consistent growth in GRP and GRP per capita indicates a resilient and expanding economy, with increased productivity and economic output per individual.

Overall, San Diego County has demonstrated strong economic performance and resilience, with continuous growth in its Gross Regional Product over the analyzed period.
