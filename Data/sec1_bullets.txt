To generate the San Diego County's Gross Regional Product (GRP) report, we will analyze the provided datasets: `sec1-1_San Diego County_grp_data.csv` and `sec1-2_San Diego County_per_capita_grp_data.csv`. These datasets contain information on the GRP by industry and per capita GRP for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023.

### Analysis of San Diego County's GRP

1. **Total GRP Growth (2019-2023):**
   - In 2019, the total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a growth of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Per Capita GRP Growth (2019-2023):**
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was $73,346.92.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP increased to $94,915.87.
   - This indicates a growth of $21,568.95 in per capita GRP over the five years.

3. **Industry Contributions to GRP (2019-2023):**
   - **Government** consistently contributed the most to the GRP, with $45.19 billion in 2019 and increasing to $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - **Manufacturing** was another significant contributor, with $28.26 billion in 2019, peaking at $33.50 billion in 2022, and slightly decreasing to $31.67 billion in 2023.
   - **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services** also showed strong contributions, starting at $28.13 billion in 2019 and growing to $37.04 billion in 2023.
   - **Finance and Insurance** and **Real Estate and Rental and Leasing** sectors also showed notable growth over the period.

4. **Population Changes (2019-2023):**
   - The population of San Diego County decreased slightly from 3,330,458 in 2019 to 3,252,468 in 2023.
   - Despite the population decrease, the GRP and per capita GRP both increased, indicating economic growth and productivity improvements.

5. **Comparison with State and National Levels:**
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP growth outpaced the state and national averages, reflecting a robust local economy.
   - The county's GRP growth rate was also higher than the national average, highlighting its economic resilience and development.

### Conclusion

San Diego County has demonstrated significant economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with substantial increases in both total and per capita GRP. Key industries such as Government, Manufacturing, and Professional Services have driven this growth. Despite a slight population decline, the county's economy has shown resilience and productivity improvements, outperforming state and national averages in per capita GRP growth. This positive economic trajectory suggests a strong foundation for future development and prosperity in San Diego County.
