The Gross Regional Product (GRP) is a key economic indicator that measures the economic output of a region. For San Diego County, we will analyze the GRP data from 2019 to 2023, focusing on the total GRP, per capita GRP, and the contributions of various industries to the overall GRP.

### Total GRP Analysis (2019-2023)

1. **2019**: The total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
2. **2020**: The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion, despite the economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
3. **2021**: A significant increase in GRP was observed, reaching approximately $268.87 billion, indicating economic recovery and growth.
4. **2022**: The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
5. **2023**: The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion, reflecting sustained economic growth.

### Per Capita GRP Analysis (2019-2023)

1. **2019**: The per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
2. **2020**: The per capita GRP increased to approximately $74,278.
3. **2021**: A notable rise in per capita GRP to approximately $82,100.
4. **2022**: The per capita GRP further increased to approximately $90,557.
5. **2023**: The per capita GRP reached approximately $94,916, indicating improved economic productivity per individual.

### Industry Contributions to GRP (2023)

1. **Government**: The largest contributor with approximately $52.92 billion.
2. **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services**: Contributed approximately $37.04 billion.
3. **Manufacturing**: Added approximately $31.67 billion to the GRP.
4. **Health Care and Social Assistance**: Contributed approximately $20.21 billion.
5. **Finance and Insurance**: Contributed approximately $19.50 billion.
6. **Information**: Added approximately $14.90 billion.
7. **Real Estate and Rental and Leasing**: Contributed approximately $12.33 billion.
8. **Retail Trade**: Added approximately $16.24 billion.
9. **Accommodation and Food Services**: Contributed approximately $11.83 billion.
10. **Administrative and Support and Waste Management and Remediation Services**: Contributed approximately $11.30 billion.

### Summary

San Diego County's GRP has shown consistent growth from 2019 to 2023, with a notable increase in both total and per capita GRP. The government sector remains the largest contributor to the GRP, followed by professional services, manufacturing, and healthcare. The data reflects a robust and diverse economy with significant contributions from various sectors, indicating a healthy economic environment in San Diego County.
