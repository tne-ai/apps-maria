The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 provides insights into the economic performance of the region over these years. Here's a detailed breakdown:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a growth of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Yearly GRP Figures**:
   - **2019**: $244.28 billion
   - **2020**: $244.82 billion
   - **2021**: $268.87 billion
   - **2022**: $296.68 billion
   - **2023**: $308.71 billion

3. **Annual Growth Rates**:
   - **2019 to 2020**: The GRP increased slightly by approximately $0.54 billion, indicating a growth rate of about 0.22%. This period was marked by the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, which likely impacted economic activities.
   - **2020 to 2021**: The GRP saw a significant increase of approximately $24.05 billion, a growth rate of about 9.82%. This reflects a recovery phase as the economy began to rebound from the pandemic's initial impact.
   - **2021 to 2022**: The GRP grew by approximately $27.81 billion, a growth rate of about 10.34%. This continued the recovery trend with robust economic activities.
   - **2022 to 2023**: The GRP increased by approximately $12.03 billion, a growth rate of about 4.06%. This indicates a stabilization phase with steady growth.

4. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP also showed an upward trend over the years, reflecting both population changes and economic growth.
   - **2019**: $73,346.92
   - **2020**: $74,277.60
   - **2021**: $82,099.95
   - **2022**: $90,557.10
   - **2023**: $94,915.87

5. **Key Industries**:
   - The data indicates that key industries contributing to the GRP include Government, Manufacturing, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Health Care and Social Assistance.
   - The Government sector consistently contributed the highest dollar value to the GRP each year.

6. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's GRP per capita has been consistently higher than the national average but slightly lower than the state average in recent years.
   - The county's economic growth aligns with broader state trends, reflecting its integration into the larger California economy.

In summary, San Diego County experienced steady economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant recovery post-2020. The region's economic resilience is evident in its ability to rebound from the pandemic's impact, driven by key industries and a strong per capita GRP performance.
