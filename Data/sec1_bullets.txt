To analyze the Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County between 2019 and 2023, we will examine the data provided in the CSV files. The analysis will focus on the total GRP, the per capita GRP, and the performance of various industries over the years.

### Total GRP Analysis

1. **2019**: The total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
2. **2020**: The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion, despite the economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
3. **2021**: The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion, indicating a recovery and growth in the economy.
4. **2022**: The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
5. **2023**: The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.

### Per Capita GRP Analysis

1. **2019**: The per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
2. **2020**: The per capita GRP increased slightly to approximately $74,278.
3. **2021**: There was a notable increase in per capita GRP to approximately $82,100.
4. **2022**: The per capita GRP rose to approximately $90,557.
5. **2023**: The per capita GRP reached approximately $94,916.

### Industry Performance

- **Accommodation and Food Services**: This industry experienced fluctuations, with a decrease in 2020 due to the pandemic, followed by recovery and growth in subsequent years.
- **Administrative and Support and Waste Management and Remediation Services**: This sector showed consistent growth over the years.
- **Agriculture, Forestry, Fishing and Hunting**: This industry had a steady increase in GRP contribution.
- **Arts, Entertainment, and Recreation**: Similar to Accommodation and Food Services, this industry was impacted by the pandemic in 2020 but recovered in the following years.
- **Construction**: The construction industry showed consistent growth throughout the period.
- **Finance and Insurance**: This sector experienced significant growth, especially in 2020 and 2021.
- **Government**: The government sector remained the largest contributor to the GRP, with steady growth each year.
- **Health Care and Social Assistance**: This industry showed consistent growth, reflecting the increasing demand for healthcare services.
- **Information**: The information sector saw growth, particularly in 2021 and 2022.
- **Manufacturing**: Manufacturing remained a strong contributor to the GRP, with growth observed in most years.
- **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services**: This sector showed significant growth, particularly in 2022 and 2023.
- **Real Estate and Rental and Leasing**: This industry experienced growth, especially in 2023.
- **Retail Trade**: Retail trade showed consistent growth over the years.
- **Transportation and Warehousing**: This sector experienced growth, particularly in 2022 and 2023.
- **Utilities**: The utilities sector showed moderate growth over the period.
- **Wholesale Trade**: This industry experienced growth, especially in 2022 and 2023.

### Conclusion

Overall, San Diego County's GRP showed a positive trend from 2019 to 2023, with both total and per capita GRP increasing each year. The recovery from the pandemic was evident in the growth observed in 2021 and beyond. Various industries contributed to this growth, with significant performances from sectors like Government, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Manufacturing.
