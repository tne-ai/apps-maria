The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the years. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Population: 3,330,458
   - GRP per Capita: $73,346.92

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Population: 3,296,045
   - GRP per Capita: $74,277.60
   - Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County's GRP saw a slight increase. The GRP per capita also increased, indicating that the economic output per person improved even as the population decreased slightly.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Population: 3,274,954
   - GRP per Capita: $82,099.95
   - The GRP saw a significant increase, reflecting a strong economic recovery post-pandemic. The GRP per capita also rose substantially, suggesting enhanced productivity and economic output per individual.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Population: 3,276,208
   - GRP per Capita: $90,557.10
   - The growth trend continued with a notable increase in both total GRP and GRP per capita, indicating sustained economic expansion and increased economic activity in the region.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Population: 3,252,468
   - GRP per Capita: $94,915.87
   - The GRP reached its highest point in this period, with a continued rise in GRP per capita. This suggests that the county's economy has been growing robustly, with increased efficiency and productivity.

**Key Observations**:
- Over the five-year period, San Diego County's GRP increased by approximately 26.4%, from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023.
- The GRP per capita rose by about 29.4%, from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023, indicating that the economic output per person has improved significantly.
- The population showed a slight decline from 2019 to 2023, which, combined with the rising GRP, suggests increased economic productivity and efficiency.

Overall, San Diego County has demonstrated strong economic growth and resilience, with significant improvements in both total GRP and GRP per capita over the analyzed period.
