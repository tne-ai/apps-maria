The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the years. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Population: 3,330,458
   - GRP per Capita: $73,346.92

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Population: 3,296,045
   - GRP per Capita: $74,277.60
   - Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, the GRP remained stable with a slight increase in per capita GRP, indicating resilience in the economy.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Population: 3,274,954
   - GRP per Capita: $82,099.95
   - The economy showed a significant recovery with a notable increase in both total GRP and per capita GRP, reflecting a strong rebound from the pandemic's impact.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Population: 3,276,208
   - GRP per Capita: $90,557.10
   - Continued economic growth is evident with a substantial rise in GRP and per capita GRP, suggesting robust economic activities and investments.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Population: 3,252,468
   - GRP per Capita: $94,915.87
   - The upward trend persists, with GRP reaching over $300 billion and per capita GRP nearing $95,000, indicating a thriving economic environment.

**Key Observations**:
- Over the five-year period, San Diego County's GRP increased by approximately 26.4%, from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023.
- The GRP per capita also saw a significant rise of about 29.4%, from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023.
- The consistent growth in GRP and per capita GRP suggests a resilient and expanding economy, with potential factors including increased productivity, investments, and a diversified economic base.

Overall, San Diego County's economic performance from 2019 to 2023 reflects a positive trajectory, with strong recovery and growth post-pandemic.
