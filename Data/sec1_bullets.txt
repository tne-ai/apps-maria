The Gross Regional Product (GRP) report for San Diego County provides an overview of the economic performance of the region over the past few years. The GRP is a measure of the total economic output of a region, similar to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) at the national level. It includes the value of all goods and services produced within the region.

### Key Highlights:

1. **Overall GRP Growth:**
   - In 2019, the GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - The GRP slightly increased to $244.82 billion in 2020, despite the economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - A significant growth was observed in 2021, with the GRP reaching approximately $268.87 billion.
   - The upward trend continued in 2022, with the GRP rising to approximately $296.68 billion.
   - In 2023, the GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.

2. **Per Capita GRP:**
   - The per capita GRP in San Diego County was $73,347 in 2019.
   - It increased to $74,278 in 2020.
   - In 2021, the per capita GRP rose to $82,100.
   - By 2022, it further increased to $90,557.
   - In 2023, the per capita GRP reached $94,916.

3. **Comparison with State and National Levels:**
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP has consistently been higher than the national average but slightly lower than the state average.
   - In 2023, the national per capita GRP was $77,366, while California's was $93,800.

4. **Industry Contributions:**
   - The Government sector was the largest contributor to the GRP, with $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector also played a significant role, contributing $37.04 billion in 2023.
   - The Manufacturing sector contributed $31.67 billion in 2023.
   - Other notable sectors include Health Care and Social Assistance ($20.21 billion) and Finance and Insurance ($19.50 billion).

5. **Trends and Observations:**
   - The GRP growth in San Diego County reflects a robust economic recovery post-pandemic, with significant contributions from key sectors such as Government, Professional Services, and Manufacturing.
   - The increase in per capita GRP indicates an improvement in economic productivity and living standards in the region.

Overall, San Diego County has demonstrated strong economic resilience and growth, with a diverse range of industries contributing to its economic output. The region's GRP performance highlights its importance as a key economic hub in California.
