The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the years. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Population: 3,330,458
   - GRP per Capita: $73,346.92

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Population: 3,296,045
   - GRP per Capita: $74,277.60
   - Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, the GRP slightly increased, and the GRP per capita also saw a modest rise due to a decrease in population.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Population: 3,274,954
   - GRP per Capita: $82,099.95
   - The economy rebounded significantly in 2021, with a notable increase in GRP and GRP per capita, reflecting recovery and growth post-pandemic.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Population: 3,276,208
   - GRP per Capita: $90,557.10
   - The growth trend continued in 2022, with both total GRP and GRP per capita showing substantial increases, indicating strong economic performance.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Population: 3,252,468
   - GRP per Capita: $94,915.87
   - The GRP reached its highest point in 2023, with a further increase in GRP per capita, suggesting continued economic expansion and productivity improvements.

**Key Observations**:
- Over the five-year period, San Diego County's GRP grew by approximately 26.4%, from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023.
- The GRP per capita increased by about 29.4%, from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023, indicating that the economic growth outpaced population growth.
- The data reflects a resilient economy that managed to recover and grow despite the global challenges posed by the pandemic, with significant contributions from various sectors driving this growth.

Overall, San Diego County's economic performance from 2019 to 2023 demonstrates robust growth and resilience, with increasing productivity and economic output per capita.
