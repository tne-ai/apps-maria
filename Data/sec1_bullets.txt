### Key Observations

1. **Growth in Total GRP**: San Diego County's total GRP has shown a consistent increase from 2019 to 2023. The GRP grew from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023, indicating a robust economic expansion over these years.

2. **Per Capita GRP Increase**: The GRP per capita also increased significantly, from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023. This suggests that the economic growth outpaced population growth, leading to higher economic output per resident.

3. **Impact of 2020**: Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, San Diego County's GRP did not decline. Instead, it saw a slight increase from 2019, indicating resilience in the county's economy.

4. **Strong Recovery and Growth Post-2020**: The years following 2020 saw substantial growth in both total GRP and per capita GRP, with the most significant increases occurring between 2020 and 2021, and continuing through 2023.

San Diego County's economy has demonstrated strong growth and resilience over the past five years.
