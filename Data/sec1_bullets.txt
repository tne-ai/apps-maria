The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the years. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Population: 3,330,458
   - GRP per Capita: $73,346.92

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Population: 3,296,045
   - GRP per Capita: $74,277.60
   - Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County's GRP saw a slight increase, reflecting resilience in certain sectors.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Population: 3,274,954
   - GRP per Capita: $82,099.95
   - The GRP increased significantly, indicating a strong recovery from the pandemic's economic impact. This growth can be attributed to the rebound in sectors such as Accommodation and Food Services, and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Population: 3,276,208
   - GRP per Capita: $90,557.10
   - The GRP continued to grow, with a notable increase in per capita GRP, suggesting improved productivity and economic output per resident.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Population: 3,252,468
   - GRP per Capita: $94,915.87
   - The GRP reached its highest point in this period, with a continued increase in per capita GRP, indicating sustained economic growth and development.

**Key Observations**:
- The GRP of San Diego County has shown a consistent increase from 2019 to 2023, with a total growth of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.
- The GRP per capita has also increased significantly, reflecting not only population changes but also improvements in economic productivity and output.
- The economic resilience and recovery post-pandemic are evident in the substantial growth observed in 2021 and 2022.
- Key sectors contributing to this growth include Government, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Manufacturing.

Overall, San Diego County's economy has demonstrated robust growth and resilience, with significant contributions from various sectors driving the GRP upward over the analyzed period.
