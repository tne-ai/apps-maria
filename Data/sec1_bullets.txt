The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over this period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total growth of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly by approximately $0.54 billion, reaching $244.82 billion.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP saw a more significant increase of approximately $24.05 billion, reaching $268.87 billion.
   - From 2021 to 2022, the GRP increased by approximately $27.81 billion, reaching $296.68 billion.
   - From 2022 to 2023, the GRP increased by approximately $12.03 billion, reaching $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This represents a growth of approximately $21,569 in per capita GRP over the five-year period.

4. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) is higher than the state average of California ($93,800) and significantly higher than the national average ($77,366).
   - This indicates that San Diego County's economic performance per individual is stronger compared to both the state and national levels.

5. **Industry Contributions**:
   - Key industries contributing to the GRP include Government, Manufacturing, and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services.
   - The Government sector consistently contributed the highest to the GRP, with approximately $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - Manufacturing and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services also showed strong contributions, with $31.67 billion and $37.04 billion respectively in 2023.

Overall, San Diego County's economy has shown resilience and growth over the past five years, with significant contributions from key industries and a per capita GRP that outpaces both state and national averages.
