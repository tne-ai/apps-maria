The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 provides insights into the economic performance of the region over these years. Here's a detailed breakdown:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a growth of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - **2019 to 2020**: The GRP increased slightly from $244.28 billion to $244.82 billion, indicating a modest growth despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - **2020 to 2021**: The GRP saw a significant increase to $268.87 billion, reflecting a recovery phase as the economy began to rebound.
   - **2021 to 2022**: The GRP continued to grow, reaching $296.68 billion, showing strong economic performance.
   - **2022 to 2023**: The GRP further increased to $308.71 billion, maintaining the growth trajectory.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP rose to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates an increase in economic productivity per person in the region.

4. **Industry Contributions**:
   - The Government sector consistently contributed the most to the GRP, with its share increasing from approximately $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector also showed significant growth, contributing approximately $28.13 billion in 2019 and increasing to $37.04 billion in 2023.
   - The Manufacturing sector remained a strong contributor, with its GRP rising from approximately $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023.

5. **Economic Resilience**:
   - Despite the economic disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County demonstrated resilience with a steady recovery and growth in GRP from 2020 onwards.
   - The diversification of industries, including strong performances in government, professional services, and manufacturing, contributed to the region's economic stability and growth.

In summary, San Diego County's GRP analysis from 2019 to 2023 highlights a period of economic growth and resilience, with significant contributions from key industries and an increase in per capita productivity.
