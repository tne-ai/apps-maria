The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the years. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total growth of about $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Yearly GRP Figures**:
   - **2019**: $244.28 billion
   - **2020**: $244.82 billion
   - **2021**: $268.87 billion
   - **2022**: $296.68 billion
   - **2023**: $308.71 billion

3. **Annual Growth Rates**:
   - **2019 to 2020**: The GRP increased slightly by approximately $0.54 billion, indicating a growth rate of about 0.22%. This minimal growth can be attributed to the economic impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - **2020 to 2021**: The GRP saw a significant increase of approximately $24.05 billion, a growth rate of about 9.82%, as the economy began to recover from the pandemic.
   - **2021 to 2022**: The GRP increased by approximately $27.81 billion, a growth rate of about 10.34%, continuing the recovery trend.
   - **2022 to 2023**: The GRP grew by approximately $12.03 billion, a growth rate of about 4.06%, indicating a stabilization in growth.

4. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP also showed an upward trend, reflecting not only overall economic growth but also an increase in economic output per individual.
   - **2019**: $73,346.92
   - **2020**: $74,277.60
   - **2021**: $82,099.95
   - **2022**: $90,557.10
   - **2023**: $94,915.87

5. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP consistently remained above the national average throughout the period.
   - In 2023, San Diego County's per capita GRP was $94,915.87, compared to California's $93,799.67 and the national average of $77,366.43.

In summary, San Diego County's economy demonstrated resilience and growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant recovery post-pandemic and a strong per capita GRP performance relative to state and national levels.
