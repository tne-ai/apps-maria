The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County provides a comprehensive overview of the economic performance of the region. The GRP is a measure of the total economic output of the region, reflecting the value of goods and services produced. Here is a detailed analysis based on the provided data:

### Overview of San Diego County's GRP (2019-2023)

1. **Total GRP Growth:**
   - In 2019, the GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a growth of approximately 26.4% over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Figures:**
   - 2019: $244.28 billion
   - 2020: $244.82 billion
   - 2021: $268.87 billion
   - 2022: $296.68 billion
   - 2023: $308.71 billion

3. **Per Capita GRP:**
   - The per capita GRP in 2019 was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates a significant increase in economic output per person, reflecting improved productivity and economic conditions.

4. **Industry Contributions:**
   - The largest contributors to the GRP in 2023 were:
     - Government: $52.92 billion
     - Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services: $37.04 billion
     - Manufacturing: $31.67 billion
     - Health Care and Social Assistance: $20.21 billion
     - Finance and Insurance: $19.50 billion

5. **Trends and Observations:**
   - The Government sector consistently remained the largest contributor to the GRP throughout the period.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector showed significant growth, reflecting the region's strength in innovation and technology.
   - The Manufacturing sector also demonstrated robust performance, contributing significantly to the GRP.
   - The Health Care and Social Assistance sector's growth highlights the increasing demand for healthcare services in the region.

6. **Comparison with State and National Levels:**
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) was higher than the state average of California ($93,800) and significantly higher than the national average ($77,366).
   - This indicates that San Diego County is performing well economically compared to both the state and national levels.

### Conclusion

San Diego County's economy has shown strong growth over the past five years, with significant contributions from the Government, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Manufacturing sectors. The increase in per capita GRP suggests improved economic productivity and prosperity for the region's residents. The county's economic performance surpasses both state and national averages, highlighting its robust economic environment.
