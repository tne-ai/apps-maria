### San Diego County GRP Analysis (2019-2023)

#### Overview
The Gross Regional Product (GRP) is a measure of the economic output of a region. It is similar to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) but at a regional level. The GRP data for San Diego County is available from 2019 to 2023, and it provides insights into the economic performance of the county over these years.

#### GRP Data Summary
- **2019**: $244.28 billion
- **2020**: $244.82 billion
- **2021**: $268.87 billion
- **2022**: $296.68 billion
- **2023**: $308.71 billion

#### GRP Per Capita
- **2019**: $73,346.92
- **2020**: $74,277.60
- **2021**: $82,099.95
- **2022**: $90,557.10
- **2023**: $94,915.87

#### Analysis
1. **Growth Trend**: San Diego County's GRP has shown a consistent upward trend from 2019 to 2023. The GRP increased from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023, indicating a strong economic growth over the five-year period.

2. **Per Capita Growth**: The GRP per capita also increased significantly, from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023. This suggests that the economic growth has outpaced population growth, leading to higher economic output per person.

3. **Yearly Growth Rates**:
   - **2019 to 2020**: The GRP remained relatively stable with a slight increase of 0.22%.
   - **2020 to 2021**: A significant increase of 9.82% in GRP, reflecting economic recovery and growth post-pandemic.
   - **2021 to 2022**: The GRP grew by 10.34%, continuing the positive trend.
   - **2022 to 2023**: The growth rate was 4.06%, indicating a steady economic expansion.

4. **Comparison with State and National Data**:
   - **State (California)**: The GRP per capita for California was $75,788.64 in 2019 and increased to $93,799.67 in 2023. San Diego County's GRP per capita growth is in line with the state trend, although slightly lower in absolute terms.
   - **National**: The national GRP per capita was $63,753.57 in 2019 and increased to $77,366.43 in 2023. San Diego County's GRP per capita is consistently higher than the national average, indicating a relatively stronger economic performance.

#### Conclusion
San Diego County has experienced robust economic growth from 2019 to 2023, as evidenced by the increase in both total GRP and GRP per capita. The county's economic performance is strong compared to national averages and aligns closely with state trends. This growth reflects the county's resilience and capacity to expand its economic output, benefiting its residents with higher per capita economic contributions.
