The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the years. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total growth of about $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly from $244.28 billion to $244.82 billion, showing resilience despite the global economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - The GRP saw a more significant increase from 2020 to 2021, rising to approximately $268.87 billion, indicating a recovery phase.
   - In 2022, the GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
   - The growth trend continued into 2023, with the GRP reaching approximately $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP also showed an upward trend, reflecting not only overall economic growth but also an increase in economic output per individual.
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, it increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates a significant improvement in economic productivity and prosperity per person in the region.

4. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) was higher than the national average of approximately $77,366, indicating a stronger economic performance relative to the national level.
   - Compared to the state of California, which had a per capita GRP of approximately $93,800 in 2023, San Diego County's per capita GRP was slightly higher, showcasing its robust economic standing within the state.

5. **Sectoral Contributions**:
   - Key sectors contributing to the GRP include Government, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Manufacturing, which consistently showed strong performance throughout the years.
   - The Government sector, in particular, was the largest contributor, with its GRP increasing from approximately $45.19 billion in 2019 to approximately $52.92 billion in 2023.

In summary, San Diego County's GRP analysis from 2019 to 2023 highlights a resilient and growing economy, with significant contributions from key sectors and a per capita GRP that outpaces both state and national averages.
