The Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County provides a comprehensive overview of the economic performance of the region. The data spans from 2019 to 2023, offering insights into the economic trends and growth within the county. Here's a detailed analysis:

### Overview of San Diego County's GRP (2019-2023)

1. **Total GRP Growth**:
   - **2019**: $244.28 billion
   - **2020**: $244.82 billion
   - **2021**: $268.87 billion
   - **2022**: $296.68 billion
   - **2023**: $308.71 billion

   The GRP of San Diego County has shown a consistent upward trend from 2019 to 2023, with a notable increase in 2021 and 2022. The total GRP grew by approximately 26.4% over the five-year period.

2. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - **2019**: $73,347
   - **2020**: $74,278
   - **2021**: $82,100
   - **2022**: $90,557
   - **2023**: $94,916

   The per capita GRP has also increased significantly, indicating that the economic growth is not just due to population growth but also due to increased productivity and economic activity per individual.

3. **Industry Contributions**:
   - **Government**: The largest contributor to the GRP, with a steady increase from $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services**: A significant sector, growing from $28.13 billion in 2019 to $37.04 billion in 2023.
   - **Manufacturing**: Another major sector, with contributions increasing from $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023.
   - **Health Care and Social Assistance**: Consistently growing, from $15.38 billion in 2019 to $20.21 billion in 2023.
   - **Finance and Insurance**: Notable growth from $14.67 billion in 2019 to $19.50 billion in 2023.

4. **Impact of COVID-19**:
   - The year 2020 saw a slight dip in the growth rate, likely due to the impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic. However, the economy rebounded strongly in 2021 and continued to grow in the subsequent years.

5. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) is higher than the state average of California ($93,800) and significantly higher than the national average ($77,366). This indicates a robust economic performance relative to both state and national levels.

### Conclusion

San Diego County's economy has demonstrated resilience and growth over the past five years, with significant contributions from government, professional services, manufacturing, and healthcare sectors. The increase in per capita GRP suggests enhanced productivity and economic activity, positioning the county as a strong economic region within California and the United States. The data reflects a positive economic trajectory, with potential for continued growth in the coming years.
