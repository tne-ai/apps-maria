### Gross Regional Product Analysis for San Diego County

#### Overview
The Gross Regional Product (GRP) is a measure of the economic output of a region. It is analogous to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) at the national level. The GRP data for San Diego County provides insights into the economic performance of various industries within the region.

#### GRP by Industry (2019-2023)
The GRP data for San Diego County is categorized by industry and spans from 2019 to 2023. Below is a summary of the key industries and their economic contributions in 2023:

1. **Government**: $52,922,899,790.76
2. **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services**: $37,043,140,500.56
3. **Manufacturing**: $31,669,563,550.34
4. **Health Care and Social Assistance**: $20,213,811,990.03
5. **Finance and Insurance**: $19,499,330,483.44

These industries are the top contributors to the GRP in San Diego County for 2023, with Government being the largest sector.

#### GRP Growth Over Time
The total GRP for San Diego County has shown a consistent increase over the years:

- **2019**: $244,278,846,457.50
- **2020**: $244,822,303,264.04
- **2021**: $268,873,550,391.04
- **2022**: $296,683,894,891.99
- **2023**: $308,710,843,090.86

This growth indicates a robust economic expansion in the region, with a notable increase from 2021 to 2022.

#### Per Capita GRP (2019-2023)
The per capita GRP provides a measure of the average economic output per person in the region. It is calculated by dividing the total GRP by the population.

- **2019**: $73,346.92
- **2020**: $74,277.60
- **2021**: $82,099.95
- **2022**: $90,557.10
- **2023**: $94,915.87

The per capita GRP has increased significantly over the years, reflecting both population growth and economic expansion.

#### Comparison with State and National Levels
For context, we compare San Diego County's per capita GRP with the state of California and the national average:

- **California (2023)**: $93,799.67
- **National (2023)**: $77,366.43

San Diego County's per capita GRP of $94,915.87 in 2023 is higher than both the state and national averages, indicating a strong economic performance relative to these benchmarks.

### Conclusion
San Diego County has demonstrated strong economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant contributions from the Government, Professional Services, and Manufacturing sectors. The increase in per capita GRP further highlights the region's economic prosperity, surpassing both state and national averages. This positive trend suggests a thriving economic environment in San Diego County.
