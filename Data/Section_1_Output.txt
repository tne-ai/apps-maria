The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County provides insights into the economic performance of the region over recent years. The data from the provided CSV files highlights the following key points:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**: 
   - In 2019, the GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP had increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a growth of approximately 26.4% over the five-year period.

2. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP in 2019 was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP had risen to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates a growth of approximately 29.4% in per capita GRP over the same period.

3. **Industry Contributions**:
   - The largest contributors to the GRP in 2023 were the Government sector ($52.92 billion), Manufacturing ($31.67 billion), and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services ($37.04 billion).
   - The Accommodation and Food Services industry showed significant recovery and growth, increasing from $6.53 billion in 2020 to $11.83 billion in 2023, reflecting a recovery from the pandemic impact.

4. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) was higher than the state average for California ($93,800) and significantly higher than the national average ($77,366).
   - This suggests that San Diego County is performing well economically compared to both state and national levels.

5. **Trends and Observations**:
   - The data indicates a robust economic recovery post-2020, with significant growth in key sectors such as Manufacturing and Professional Services.
   - The increase in per capita GRP suggests improvements in productivity and economic well-being for residents of San Diego County.

Overall, the GRP data for San Diego County reflects a strong and growing economy, with significant contributions from diverse industries and a per capita GRP that outpaces both state and national averages.
