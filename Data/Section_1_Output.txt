To generate a report on the Gross Regional Product (GRP) for San Diego County, we will analyze the data provided in the `sec1_San Diego County_grp_data.csv` and `sec1_San Diego County_per_capita_grp_data.csv` files. The analysis will focus on the GRP by industry and the per capita GRP over the years 2019 to 2023. We will also compare San Diego County's GRP with the state of California and the national level.

### Gross Regional Product Analysis for San Diego County

#### 1. Overview of GRP by Industry (2019-2023)

The GRP data for San Diego County is broken down by industry for each year from 2019 to 2023. Here are the key findings:

- **Government** consistently contributes the highest GRP among all industries, with a notable increase from $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
- **Manufacturing** is another significant contributor, with GRP rising from $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023.
- **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services** also show substantial growth, increasing from $28.13 billion in 2019 to $37.04 billion in 2023.
- **Health Care and Social Assistance** and **Finance and Insurance** are other major industries, with GRP values of $20.21 billion and $19.50 billion respectively in 2023.

#### 2. GRP Per Capita (2019-2023)

The GRP per capita provides insight into the economic productivity of the average individual in San Diego County:

- In 2019, the GRP per capita was $73,346.92.
- There was a steady increase over the years, reaching $94,915.87 in 2023.
- This growth indicates an improvement in economic productivity per person in the county.

#### 3. Comparison with State and National Levels

- **California (State Level):**
  - The GRP per capita for California was $75,788.64 in 2019 and increased to $93,799.67 in 2023.
  - San Diego County's GRP per capita is slightly higher than the state average in 2023, indicating a strong local economy.

- **United States (National Level):**
  - The national GRP per capita was $63,753.57 in 2019 and increased to $77,366.43 in 2023.
  - San Diego County's GRP per capita is significantly higher than the national average, highlighting the county's robust economic performance.

### Conclusion

San Diego County has shown consistent economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant contributions from the government, manufacturing, and professional services sectors. The GRP per capita has increased steadily, surpassing both the state and national averages, indicating a strong and productive local economy. This positive trend suggests that San Diego County is well-positioned for continued economic prosperity.
