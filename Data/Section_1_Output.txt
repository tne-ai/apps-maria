The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County provides insights into the economic performance of the region over the years 2019 to 2023. Here's a detailed breakdown of the data:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP had increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a growth of approximately 26.4% over the five-year period.

2. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP in 2019 was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, this figure had risen to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates a significant increase in economic productivity per person, with a growth of approximately 29.4%.

3. **Comparison with State and National Averages**:
   - In 2023, San Diego County's per capita GRP of $94,916 was higher than the California state average of $93,800 and significantly higher than the national average of $77,366.
   - This suggests that San Diego County is performing well economically compared to both the state and national levels.

4. **Industry Contributions**:
   - The largest contributors to the GRP in 2023 were Government ($52.92 billion), Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services ($37.04 billion), and Manufacturing ($31.67 billion).
   - These industries have consistently been major contributors over the years, indicating their importance to the regional economy.

5. **Trends and Observations**:
   - The Government sector has shown consistent growth, increasing from $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector also saw significant growth, from $28.13 billion in 2019 to $37.04 billion in 2023.
   - The Manufacturing sector, while experiencing fluctuations, remains a strong contributor to the GRP.

Overall, San Diego County has demonstrated robust economic growth over the past five years, with significant contributions from key industries and a per capita GRP that outpaces both state and national averages. This indicates a strong and resilient regional economy.
