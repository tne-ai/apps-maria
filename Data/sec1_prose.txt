The Gross Regional Product (GRP) is a measure of the economic output of a region, analogous to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) at the national level. The GRP data for San Diego County provides valuable insights into the region's economic performance over the years. From 2019 to 2023, San Diego County's GRP has shown a consistent increase, with a notable rise in 2021 and 2022. The total GRP grew by approximately 26.4%, rising from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023. During this period, the population of San Diego County fluctuated slightly, decreasing from 3,330,458 in 2019 to 3,252,468 in 2023. Despite this slight population decline, the GRP per capita improved significantly, increasing from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023, indicating enhanced economic output per person.

When comparing San Diego County's GRP per capita with state and national data, it becomes clear that the region's economy is strong and competitive. The state of California's GRP per capita ranged from $75,788.64 in 2019 to $93,799.67 in 2023, while the national average was lower, ranging from $63,753.57 in 2019 to $77,366.43 in 2023. San Diego County's GRP per capita consistently exceeded the national average, reflecting a robust regional economy. Although the county's GRP per capita was slightly lower than California's state average, the gap has been narrowing over the years.

In conclusion, San Diego County has demonstrated robust economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant increases in both total GRP and GRP per capita. This growth underscores the region's resilience and adaptability in the face of economic challenges, positioning San Diego County as a strong and competitive economic force within California and the nation.
