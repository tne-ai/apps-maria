San Diego County's Gross Regional Product (GRP) experienced substantial growth from 2019 to 2023, demonstrating a robust economy despite slight population fluctuations.  Total GRP increased steadily, rising from approximately $244.3 billion in 2019 to $308.7 billion in 2023.  This consistent upward trend reflects a thriving economic environment.

While the county's population decreased slightly from 3,330,458 in 2019 to 3,252,468 in 2023, the GRP per capita saw a significant jump.  In 2019, GRP per capita was $73,346.92, increasing to $94,915.87 by 2023. This substantial increase in economic output per person underscores the county's growing productivity and economic strength.

Key figures for each year highlight this trend: 2020 saw a GRP of $244.82 billion and a per capita GRP of $74,277.60; 2021 marked a GRP of $268.87 billion and a per capita GRP of $82,099.95; 2022 saw a GRP of $296.68 billion and a per capita GRP of $90,557.10.  These figures clearly illustrate the consistent growth trajectory.

In conclusion, San Diego County's economy has demonstrated remarkable resilience and growth over the past five years.  The sustained increase in both total GRP and GRP per capita, despite a slight population decrease, points to a strong and productive economic environment.
